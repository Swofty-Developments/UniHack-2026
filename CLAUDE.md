## Design Context

### Users
People with disabilities navigating physical spaces (wheelchair users, low vision, limited mobility, hearing impaired, neurodivergent, elderly, parents with prams). They need to feel **empowered and confident** — the app should make them feel capable and in control of their environment, not patronized. Secondary users are campus community members contributing accessibility data through scanning.

### Brand Personality
**Warm, inclusive, playful.** The tone is encouraging and community-driven, not clinical or corporate. The gamification (territories, leaderboards, scanning) should feel rewarding without trivializing the accessibility mission.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Dark mode only. Deep navy/charcoal backgrounds (`#0B0B14`, `#161625`) with teal primary (`#00D4AA`) and amber accent (`#FFB547`)
- **Effects**: Glassmorphism, glow effects, floating elements. The UI should feel modern and polished but warm — not cold/cyberpunk
- **References**: Strava / Nike Run Club — community-driven activity tracking with leaderboards, achievements, and a sense of personal progress. Emphasis on celebrating contributions and making data feel personal
- **Anti-references**: Avoid clinical/medical aesthetics, overly corporate accessibility tools, or cold/sterile UIs
- **Typography**: Bold weights for headers, clean sans-serif, generous spacing
- **Icons**: Ionicons throughout, filled variants for active states

### Design Principles
1. **Empowerment over assistance** — Frame features as giving users power, not helping the helpless. Language and visuals should convey capability and independence.
2. **Celebrate contributions** — Every scan, territory claim, and hazard report should feel like a meaningful achievement. Use animation and feedback to reward engagement.
3. **Clarity at a glance** — Map data, hazard severity, and navigation info must be instantly readable. Use color coding (red/amber/green for hazards) and strong visual hierarchy.
4. **Warm technology** — Glassmorphism and glow effects should feel inviting, not dystopian. Soften hard edges with rounded corners and subtle gradients.
5. **Accessible by default** — Adequate touch targets, clear contrast on all text, screen reader labels on interactive elements. Best-effort WCAG AA for the hackathon demo.
